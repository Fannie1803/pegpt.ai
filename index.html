Education

PE-GPT supports AI-native power electronics education through open resources, project-based learning, and engineering-ready deliverables.

In the midst of the rapid advancement of artificial intelligence, it is time to prepare the next generation of the power electronics workforce. Future power electronics engineers and researchers will be distinguished not only by their domain expertise, but also by their ability to work with AI as an engineering collaborator.

As an agentic framework, PE-GPT provides educational resources freely through its open-source release on GitHub. It enables the training of students to develop an interdisciplinary mindset and to effectively harness AI capabilities in solving real-world power electronics engineering problems.

Educational Deployment & Outcomes

Through educational efforts in collaboration with the University of Arkansas Power Group, the PE-GPT framework has been deployed in a graduate-level course on AI for power electronics design. Within a 16-week semester, postgraduate students with zero prior AI expertise independently built, extended, and demonstrated functional PE-GPT-based solutions, emphasizing task decomposition, verification, and engineering-ready deliverables.
